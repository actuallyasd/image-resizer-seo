    Overall context

    Date range analysed: Nov 13 – Dec 18, 2025
    Paid ads paused. Focus on Organic, Referral, Email discovery and early product usage.

    Traffic Acquisition – High-level insights

    • Traffic is still very low overall, which is expected at this stage. Absolute numbers matter less than signal quality.
    • Referral is currently our strongest channel in terms of volume and meaningful engagement.
    • Organic Search is effectively zero right now → this is not a red flag, just confirmation that SEO has not started compounding yet.
    • Organic Social exists, but volume is extremely small and sporadic.

    Interpretation:

    • We are still in the distribution seeding phase, not the growth phase.
    • Directories + communities are doing more work than search engines today.

    Referral traffic – what’s working

    Top referral sources observed:

    • indiehackers.com → highest volume + decent engagement
    • startuptile.com → volume present but low engagement (likely curiosity clicks)
    • manage.wix.com → internal / builder-related traffic (not true acquisition)
    • tagassistant.google.com → developer/debug traffic, ignore for growth analysis

    Key takeaways:

    • IndieHacker-style communities are high-intent. So posting more on Indie Hackers and similar communities.
    • Some directory traffic brings volume but not depth → still useful for awareness.
    • Internal / tooling referrals should be excluded when judging traction.

    Events & product usage (most important signal)

    Events observed (core funnel):

    • page_view
    • image_upload
    • export_images
    • download_zip

    Insights:

    • Users are not just bouncing — uploads and exports are happening.
    • Drop-off exists between upload → export → download, which is normal.
    • Very few users reach advanced actions (edit_export, download_assets).

    Interpretation:

    • You already have product–problem fit for the users who arrive.
    • The bottleneck is traffic quality & volume, not UX fundamentals.

    Retention overview – what’s really happening

    Headline numbers:

    • New users: 195
    • Returning users: 38 (~19–20%)

    What this means:

    • For a utility tool, this is actually not bad early-stage retention.
    • Most users are single-use by nature, so retention will always be capped.

    User retention curve:

    • Sharp drop after Day 1 → normal for utilities
    • Very small but real Day 7 returns → signal that some users bookmark / reuse. In the future maybe adding a tiny feature after they have resized their images that asks them to bookmark the website? It can be a good retention strategy.

    Stickiness (DAU / WAU / MAU)

    Observed:

    • DAU / MAU ≈ 28%
    • DAU / WAU ≈ 35.8%
    • WAU / MAU ≈ 78%

    Interpretation:

    • WAU/MAU is inflated due to low volume. So even if it looks good we should not take it as a great signal. Studying it over a longer priod of time would make more sense.
    • DAU ratios suggest bursty usage, not daily habit. This is more or less expected for our tool where one time usage is more prevalent.

    What is NOT a problem (important)

    • Low Organic Search → expected
    • Low retention curve → expected for utilities
    • No revenue → expected at this phase

    None of these indicate failure.

    What IS the real signal to act on

    • Referral channels are working. We should continue to prioritise and double down on the following:
      • Directories
      • Emails and other referrals
      • Tool roundups
    • Users who arrive do use the product → distribution is the constraint
    • SEO needs time + motive-driven landing pages (which you’re already building)

    Plain-English insights from the reports

    • Your December Search campaign is doing almost all the work. The Europe campaign is tiny by comparison, so it will look “weird” just because the sample size is small.
    • Traffic quality is strong. People who arrive are not instantly bouncing — they’re engaging and triggering actions.
    • Europe traffic quality isn’t worse — it’s just lower volume. The issue is reach/scale, not relevance.
    • The big drop after “App redirect” is expected. Users move from Wix to your Firebase tool — that handoff is a real step change in the journey.
    • The real bottleneck is activation (getting users to actually start using the tool). You want more people to upload an image after landing, not just click “Try for free”.
    • Right now Google Ads is optimising for the conversion you gave it. If “app_redirect” is the conversion, the system will try to maximise button clicks — not image uploads.
    • You won’t get historical keyword → image_upload performance if you change conversions today. The past stays tied to the old conversion. Changing the conversion affects learning going forward.
    • Retention is naturally capped for a utility tool. Many users are one-and-done; the real win is the small group who come back and reuse it.

    One-line takeaway: You don’t have a traffic problem — you have an activation + measurement problem.
